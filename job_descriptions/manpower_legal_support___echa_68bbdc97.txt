Legal support - ECHA

The European Chemicals Agency (ECHA) is the driving force among regulatory authorities in implementing the EU's groundbreaking chemicals legislation for the benefit of human health and the environment as well as for innovation and competitiveness. ECHA helps companies to comply with the legislation, advances the safe use of chemicals, provides information on chemicals and addresses chemicals of concern. https://echa.europa.eu/about-us

Manpower is the primary workforce agency to provide interim professionals for The European Chemicals Agency (ECHA).

Now we are looking for LEGAL SUPPORTS to The European Chemicals Agency (ECHA) with this open application model.

We welcome open applications from motivated and qualified candidates. By submitting your application, you allow us to consider you for any upcoming roles that match your skills and experience. We will reach out to you if a fitting opportunity opens up.

Whether you have prior experience with ECHA or are a new candidate, we warmly welcome your application through us.

Tasks/Duties:
Legal support to cover assignments that provide support in assisting lawyers in the area of legal advice, legal research and litigation as well as providing assistance in legal analysis, in legal documentation and in drafting notes relevant to the work of the unit.

Requirements:

Bachelor degrees in Law
Good English skills both written and spoken (as this is the working language of the Agency)

What we offer:
We offer you an interesting, multicultural working environment and a chance to develop your skills.
ECHA's work environment is very international and collaborative and the office is located in Helsinki city center.
The positions are variable fixed-term and full-time temporary employment relationships through Manpower and always depend on needs and mandates.

How to register:
Register yourself for this open application model via Manpower's link and add your EUROPASS CV in English as soon as possible.

For GDPR reasons, we do not accept applications or CVs via email.

In case you don't have an EUROPASS CV, please use this: https://europa.eu/europass/eportfolio/screen/cv-editor?lang=en

We will contact you if a suitable position opens up through us.
