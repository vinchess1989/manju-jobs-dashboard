Health & Safety Advisors

Mission-Critical Roofing, Cladding & Façade Projects

Project Locations: Germany | Denmark | Sweden | Finland

Working Arrangement: Rotation-Based | International Travel Across Europe

Role Overview

We are seeking multiple Health & Safety Advisors to join our growing European operations team. Applications are welcome from candidates currently based across Europe who are interested in working on high-profile, mission-critical construction projects.

The successful candidates will support the delivery of roofing, cladding, and façade packages on major projects across Germany, Denmark, Sweden, and Finland. Working closely with project managers, site teams, clients, and subcontractors, you will play a key role in ensuring that all works are delivered safely, compliantly, and in line with Crown's industry-leading standards.

This position offers the opportunity to work on some of Europe's most technically challenging projects while gaining valuable international experience within a fast-growing specialist contractor.

Key Responsibilities


Promote and drive a positive health and safety culture across all project teams
Ensure compliance with local legislation, client requirements, and Crown's health and safety management systems
Conduct regular site inspections, audits, and safety observations, identifying risks and implementing corrective actions where required
Prepare and review risk assessments, method statements, and safe systems of work
Deliver site inductions, toolbox talks, safety briefings, and ongoing workforce engagement initiatives
Support project teams in planning works safely and identifying potential hazards before activities commence
Monitor subcontractor performance and ensure adherence to project-specific health and safety requirements
Investigate incidents, accidents, and near misses, producing detailed reports and implementing corrective and preventative measures
Maintain accurate health and safety records, documentation, and reporting systems
Liaise with clients, consultants, regulatory authorities, and other stakeholders on health and safety matters
Provide advice and guidance to project management teams on best practice and continuous improvement initiatives
Support internal and external audits and contribute to the ongoing development of Crown's health and safety standards
Assist in achieving project and company health and safety objectives while ensuring all works are delivered safely, on programme, and to the highest quality standards


Requirements


Bachelor's degree in Occupational Health & Safety or related discipline, with a recognised professional qualification (NBachelor's degree in Occupational Health & Safety, Environmental Health & Safety, Construction Management, or a related discipline
Recognised Health & Safety qualification, such as NEBOSH General Certificate, NEBOSH Construction Certificate, or equivalent (NEBOSH Diploma desirable)
Minimum 3 years' experience in a Health & Safety Advisor role within the construction industry
Experience working on roofing, cladding, façade, building envelope, or large-scale construction projects is desirable
Data centre or mission-critical project experience is highly desirable
Strong working knowledge of EU and local Health & Safety legislation, regulations, and industry best practice
Proven ability to influence, coach, and engage project teams, subcontractors, and stakeholders to maintain the highest Health & Safety standards
Experience conducting site inspections, audits, incident investigations, and risk assessments
Excellent communication, reporting, and stakeholder management skills
Ability to manage multiple projects and work effectively in a fast-paced construction environment
Willingness and ability to travel and work on projects across Europe as required
Currently based in Europe and eligible to work across EU project locations
Full, clean driving licence
Proactive, highly organised, and solutions-focused, with a strong commitment to promoting a positive safety culture


Benefits


Competitive salary, commensurate with experience
Rotation-based working arrangement with international project exposure
Opportunity to work on high-profile, mission-critical construction projects across Europe
Clear career progression opportunities within a growing and ambitious organisation
Ongoing training, professional development, and support for further qualifications
Collaborative and supportive working environment with a strong focus on employee wellbeing
Opportunity to work alongside experienced industry professionals on technically challenging projects


Crown is an equal opportunities employer and welcomes applications from all suitably qualified candidates.
