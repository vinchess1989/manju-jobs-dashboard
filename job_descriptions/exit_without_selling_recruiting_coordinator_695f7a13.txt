THE ROLE

Most business owners don't have time to build recruiting systems. That's the problem EWS solves — and you're the person who makes the delivery happen.

Exit Without Selling builds Opportunity Recruiting Funnels for small business owners: structured 12-asset systems that take a client from zero to a live job posting with a fully configured hiring pipeline. Every build follows a defined sequence. Every asset goes through a quality pass. Every client gets trained before the posting goes live.

The Opportunity Recruiting Coordinator owns the entire build from the moment the advisor says go. That means account setup, running the build in Claude, editing for quality, configuring the applicant tracking system, running the kickoff call with the client, publishing the posting, and creating all funnel stages. The advisor handles client relationships and strategy. You handle everything else.

What The Work Actually Looks Like

You work async, primarily through Slack. Builds arrive with a Team IV and a job description or Success Profile attached. From there, you execute a defined sequence without being followed up with. The advisor's next message from you should be a Slack confirming the ORF is live — not a question about where to start.

Each Build Involves


Creating the client's ATS (applicant tracking system) account and Whop welcome package on the same day the build is triggered
Running the full 12-asset playbook build in Claude using the provided inputs — no improvising, no shortcuts
Applying the anti-AI editing pass across all 12 assets, two at a time, before anything moves to Drive
Saving the ATS job posting as a draft — not publishing — before the kickoff call
Running the kickoff call: comp, location, wording, interview process, email templates, ATS training, applicant filtering
Publishing the posting live during the kickoff call after the client signs off
Creating all eight funnel stages in ATS in order after the posting is live
Slacking the advisor when — and only when — every step is done


THIS IS NOT FOR YOU IF

You need check-ins to stay on track. You interpret a build SOP as a starting point rather than a sequence. You'd publish a posting before reviewing it with the client. You'd reach out to the client when something is missing instead of going back to the advisor. You'd finish the kickoff call before confirming the client can work the ATS independently.

The sequence exists for a reason. Steps that happen out of order create problems downstream — for the client, for the advisor, and for the build quality. If following a defined process precisely is something you'd push back on, this isn't the right fit.

Who Belongs Here

You've done this kind of work before in a different context — operations, executive assistant, recruiting coordinator, project management. You know what it means to own an outcome without being managed through it. You're comfortable on client calls and don't need a script to run a structured agenda.

You're drawn to work that has a clear right answer. The build SOP says do this, then this, then this — and you find that clarifying, not limiting. You catch your own mistakes before they ship. You communicate once with specifics and wait, rather than sending vague follow-up messages.

You understand that the coordinator role exists to take execution completely off the advisor's plate. That's the job. Not most of it — all of it.

What You Need


Reliable async availability during US business hours for client kickoff calls
Comfort running structured calls with business owner clients
Ability to work in Slack, Google Drive, Claude, and the ATS — or learn the ATS quickly
Strong written communication — you're editing recruiting copy and communicating with clients
Attention to sequence: this role runs a defined process, not a flexible workflow


You Do NOT Need


Prior recruiting industry experience
A degree
Prior ATS experience — platform training is provided


PAY AND ENGAGEMENT

$20–$40/hr contractor rate, set at offer based on experience and execution quality. Volume-based builds are also possible for coordinators who demonstrate consistent quality at pace. No benefits, no fixed hours, project-based availability.

THE PROCESS


Apply (5 minutes)
15-minute phone screen if your background matches
Written character questions sent by email — reply at your own pace
45-minute interview focused on how you've handled process-execution work in the past
Paid trial build — one ORF build, evaluated against the quality standard


If you've read this far and the sequence-based work sounds clarifying rather than limiting, apply. That response is already a signal.
