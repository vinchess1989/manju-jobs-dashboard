Team Member Relations Partner

The Team Member Relations Partner supports the Team Member Relations function by promoting a positive, respectful, and compliant workplace environment across CAVA’s restaurant teams. This role partners closely with People + Culture Business Partners, Operations leaders, and team members to address workplace concerns, support investigations, provide guidance on company policies, and help ensure consistent application of employment practices.




The Team Member Relations Partner serves as a trusted resource for team members and leaders while helping foster an inclusive culture aligned with CAVA’s values. This position requires strong judgment, professionalism, and the ability to manage sensitive situations with confidentiality and care.




This job description is not intended to be a comprehensive list of all duties and responsibilities of the position, and such duties and responsibilities may change without notice.




Success at CAVA: At CAVA, Team Member Relations Parnters are proactive problem-solvers who build strong relationships, deliver excellent service, and contribute to a positive team member experience. We look for individuals who demonstrate sound judgment, communicate effectively, embrace learning, and support a culture of fairness, accountability, and belonging.




What You'll Do:

Serve as a first point of contact for team member relations concerns and workplace issues.
Conduct intake conversations and support investigations involving policy violations, workplace conflicts, performance concerns, and other employee relations matters.
Document investigations, findings, and recommendations in a thorough, timely, and objective manner.
Partner with People + Culture Business Partners and Operations leaders to resolve team member concerns and support appropriate corrective actions.
Provide guidance to leaders on company policies, procedures, and best practices for team member management.
Support the administration and tracking of team member relations cases through case management systems and reporting tools.
Monitor trends in team member relations data and identify opportunities for proactive interventions and process improvements.
Assist in developing and delivering training related to workplace conduct, respectful workplace expectations, and company policies.
Support compliance with federal, state, and local employment laws and company policies.
Collaborate with cross-functional partners, including Legal, Payroll, Learning + Development, and HRIS, as needed to support team member relations initiatives.
Maintain confidentiality and exercise sound judgment when handling sensitive information.
Contribute to projects and initiatives that enhance the overall team member experience and strengthen workplace culture.




Qualifications:




Education & Experience




2–3 years of experience in Human Resources, Employee Relations, Labor Relations, or a related field, preferably in the restaurant or hospitality industry.
Experience supporting workplace investigations, conflict resolution, or employee relations matters preferred.
Working knowledge of employment laws and HR best practices.
Strong interpersonal and communication skills with the ability to build trust and credibility at all levels of the organization.
Demonstrated ability to manage multiple priorities in a fast-paced environment.
Excellent organizational skills and attention to detail.
Strong critical thinking, problem-solving, and decision-making capabilities.
Ability to handle confidential information with professionalism and discretion.
Proficiency in Microsoft Office Suite and HR systems.
Bachelor's degree in Human Resources, Business Administration, Psychology, Communications, or a related field preferred.
