Overview
A global, privately held manufacturer of engineered materials is seeking an
M&A Counsel
to support its active growth strategy. This role will partner closely with senior leadership on domestic and cross-border acquisitions, divestitures, and strategic investments, while also advising on broader corporate matters.
*This is a contract-to-hire opportunity. While the position is remote, candidates must be located in Georgia.
Key Responsibilities
Lead legal support for M&A transactions, including acquisitions, divestitures, joint ventures, and minority investments
Draft, review, and negotiate transaction documents (LOIs, NDAs, purchase agreements, disclosure schedules, ancillary agreements)
Manage due diligence processes, coordinating with internal stakeholders and external advisors
Advise on transaction structuring, risk allocation, and integration considerations
Support post-closing integration activities and ongoing governance matters
Partner with business and finance teams on strategic initiatives and corporate development efforts
Oversee and manage external counsel to ensure efficient deal execution
Qualifications
J.D. from an accredited law school and active bar membership
5-10+ years of M&A experience, preferably from a top law firm and/or in-house with a multinational company
Strong experience handling complex, cross-border transactions
Background supporting manufacturing, industrial, or materials-based businesses preferred
Demonstrated ability to manage deals independently and operate in a fast-paced environment
Why This Role
Opportunity to take a leading role in a high-volume, global M&A pipeline
Direct exposure to senior leadership and strategic decision-making
Broad scope across corporate, transactional, and operational legal matters within a growth-oriented organization
