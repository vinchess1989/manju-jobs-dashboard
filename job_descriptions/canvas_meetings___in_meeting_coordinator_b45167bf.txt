Meeting Coordinator




Location: Remote

Reports to: Director, Event Operations

Preferred Start Date: Aug 5, 2026

Salary Range: $53,000–$58,000 plus benefits




About Canvas

Canvas Meetings & Incentives is a woman-owned business specializing in full-service event planning. With over 27 years of experience, we create transformative events while prioritizing inclusivity, sustainability, and collaboration.




About the Role

The Meeting Coordinator is a detail-oriented, highly organized professional who supports the planning and execution of meetings, conferences, and events. This role requires thoughtful communication skills, customer service excellence, and a proactive approach to problem-solving. If you thrive in a fast-paced, remote environment and are passionate about event coordination, this is the role for you.

What You'll Do

Collaborate with the Lead Planner on various aspects of the project scope of work, including but not limited to:

Designing and managing registration websites for client events, utilizing conditional logic for a seamless user experience.
Overseeing event registration, including accommodations, travel requests, dietary restrictions, and personal data collection.
Verifying rooming lists, flight manifests, and ground transportation records for accuracy.
Serving as the primary point of contact for attendees, providing program details and addressing inquiries.
Coordinating directly with air and ground transportation providers to arrange seamless travel for attendees.
Sourcing, negotiating, and managing a diverse range of suppliers to meet program-specific needs.
Supporting the planning and execution of both on-site and off-site event logistics to ensure flawless execution.
Monitoring the varying components of event operations to uphold client expectations and enhance attendee satisfaction.
Reviewing and reconciling event invoices, ensuring accurate and timely vendor payments, and assisting with program financial closeout.
Representing Canvas with professionalism and expertise across all facets of the hospitality industry.
Completing additional projects as specified by the Director, Event Operations.




What You Bring

Required Skills & Experience

Minimum of 3 years of experience in hospitality, meetings, events, travel coordination, or a related field.
Demonstrated experience managing attendee travel, rooming lists, and ground transportation logistics.
Proficiency in Excel, Google Suite, CRM systems, and event technology platforms.
Experience supporting multiple projects and priorities simultaneously in a fast-paced environment.




Skills for Success

Exceptional Organization: Highly skilled in managing details, timelines, and competing priorities with accuracy and efficiency.
Superior Customer Service: Committed to delivering high-touch, professional service with a client-first mindset.
Attention to Detail: Able to manage multiple projects across different industries while maintaining a personalized level of service.
Hospitality Industry Expertise: Understanding of meeting coordination, attendee management, and event logistics.
Creative Problem-Solving: Innovative thinker who brings adaptable solutions and a proactive approach to challenges.
Self-Motivated & Disciplined: Thrives in a remote work environment while maintaining accountability and focus.
Collaborative Team Player: Energetic, engaged, and confident working within a dynamic, high-performing team.




What Success Looks Like in This Role

Delivering a seamless and positive experience for attendees, clients, vendors, and team members through responsive communication and thoughtful support.
Managing event logistics with accuracy and attention to detail, ensuring travel, registration, and program information are organized and up to date.
Anticipating needs, solving problems proactively, and helping projects move forward smoothly in a fast-paced environment.
Building trusted relationships through reliability, professionalism, and a commitment to exceptional service.
Contributing to a collaborative team culture by demonstrating accountability, flexibility, and a willingness to support others.




Must-Have Qualifications

Legally authorized to work in the United States
Willing to undergo a background check in accordance with local law/regulations
Willing and available to travel for programs up to twice per month, including occasional back-to-back travel during peak seasons.
Travel requirements may vary based on program needs.
Must possess a valid U.S. passport.
Comfortable working with international participants, government officials, and stakeholders from a variety of cultural backgrounds.
Understanding of international business etiquette, including the appropriate use of formal titles, diplomatic protocols, and VIP service standards.
