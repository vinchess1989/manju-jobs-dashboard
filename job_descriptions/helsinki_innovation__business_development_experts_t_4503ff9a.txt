We are seeking Business Development Experts to University of Helsinki science-based commercialization projects

 

University of Helsinki research commercialisation teams are looking for business development experts who are eager to make an impact by turning science-based inventions into successful businesses.

 

The University of Helsinki has a proven track record on innovation and business creation utilizing Research to Business funding from Business Finland. Research to Business-funded projects run for 1.5–2 years with an approximate budget of 500–800k EUR. Research to Business projects are coordinated and supported by Helsinki Innovation Services Ltd, the technology transfer and research commercialisation company of the University of Helsinki.




We expect

Ideally, you would support the research team already during the Research to Business funding preparation phase for the funding call in Fall 2026. In case of a positive funding decision, you will join the team as a Business Development Expert  (also known as Commercial Champion). We expect you to have a suitable background and experience in commercialisation and/or business, as well as some understanding of the project’s field of science. In addition to fluency in English, excellent communication, teamwork, and negotiation skills are key to master the role successfully.

 

We offer

This meaningful position comes with the unique opportunity to build a global business based on world class scientific discoveries. You will work in an international community immersed in a vibrant innovation ecosystem. You will be employed by the University of Helsinki with flexibility regarding part-time or full-time employment.

 

You will

As a Commercial Champion, you will have the overall responsibility to develop, coordinate, and implement the project’s commercialisation activities in close collaboration with the research team and Helsinki Innovation Services. Usual tasks include market analysis, participation in IPR protection, regulatory investigations, reach-out activities to future customers, partners, and investors, as well as the development of a solid business strategy.

 

The following innovation projects are looking for Business Development Experts:




Project01 | DENVAX

Project description: Our project focuses on developing a novel dendritic cell vaccine platform for glioblastoma that enhances immune activation against tumors. The goal is to create safer, more effective, and scalable immunotherapy for patients with limited treatment options.

Contact person: Prof. Susanna Fagerholm

Email to: susanna.fagerholm@helsinki.fi




Project02 | STOPAD

Project description: Alzheimer’s disease and other dementia lack effective therapies that could stop or delay the disease’s progression. The STOPAD project aims to develop novel small-molecular ligands that will target various disease mechanisms, being potentially effective enough to stop or delay the neuronal death.

Contact person: Prof. Timo Myöhänen

Email to: timo.myohanen@helsinki.fi




Project03 | GutOmics

Project description: We aim to prepare the commercialization of a combination of microbial and genetic biomarkers for inflammatory bowel disease and celiac disease diagnostics and therapy outcome prediction.

Contact person: Päivi Saavalainen

Email to: paivi.saavalainen@helsinki.fi




Project04 | Morphotype AI

Project description: Morphotype AI is building a new way to diagnose blood diseases without traditional histology. Our AI-powered platform uses advanced imaging to deliver fast, accurate insights that will help doctors make treatment decisions sooner.

Contact person: Prof. Caroline Heckman

Email to: caroline.heckman@helsinki.fi




Project05 | AtheroDx

Project description: Atherosclerosis is a slowly developing silent killer “claiming” nearly 20 million lives each year across the globe. Yet, it often goes undetected until symptoms appear. Our team is developing AhteroDx, an entirely novel molecular blood test based on world‑class clinical research that enables early diagnosis and care intervention.

Contact person: Vilbert Sikorski 

Email to: vilbert.sikorski@helsinki.fi




Project06 | SisuTx

Project description: Develops targeted radioligands for diagnostics and treatment of triple negative breast cancer and its multi-organ metastasis, including asymptomatic brain metastasis.

Contact person: Director Pirjo Laakkonen

Email to: pirjo.laakkonen@helsinki.fi




Project07 | EXHALE

Project description: We aim to commercialize a modular solid-sorbent polishing cartridge that suppresses volatile amine emissions from post-combustion CO₂ capture plants under wet and warm operating conditions. EXHALE addresses a critical permitting and public-acceptance barrier to broader carbon capture deployment, targeting industrial operators, capture technology suppliers, and EPC providers.

Contact person: Prof. Pedro Camargo

Email to: pedro.camargo@helsinki.fi




Project08 | PD-BIOTIC

Project description: Our project aims to select and characterize new potentially disease modifying bacteria for Parkinson's disease that will then be combined in a therapeutic consortium. The consortium will be tested in preclinical models to provide proof of concept for future commercialisation and therapeutic use.

Contact person: Filip Scheperjans

Email to: filip.scheperjans@helsinki.fi




Applications

Please note that these projects are currently preparing applications for the Research to Business (R2B) funding programme and have not yet received funding. As part of their preparations, the project teams are looking to identify potential Business Development Experts who would be interested in joining the projects. Projects that are successful in securing R2B funding are expected to start in early 2027. The selected Business Development Experts would then have the opportunity to join the project teams at the start of the funded project period.




Please send your application and CV by email to the contact person (research group leader) and cite the project reference in the email subject latest by 30th June 2026. Interviews of suitable candidates will start on a rolling basis depending on each contact person’s timetables.

 

Please note that direct LinkedIn applies only will not be considered.

 

We look forward to hearing from you!
