Brightline is redefining what pediatric behavioral health care looks like for families across the country — and we're looking for the organizational force multiplier who will help make that vision a daily reality.

This is a high-visibility, high-trust role at the center of Brightline's executive operations. You'll support multiple C-suite leaders — managing competing priorities, complex calendars, and confidential work across time zones with precision and discretion.

This isn't a role for someone who needs a lot of hand-holding or prefers a predictable 9-to-5. It's for someone who moves fast, thinks ahead, and takes deep ownership over keeping senior leaders focused on what matters most.

Why This Role

Brightline is at an inflection point — scaling our clinical programs, expanding into new markets, and deepening our impact on kids and families who need us. The executives you'll support are driving that growth every single day, and you'll be the connective tissue that keeps everything running.

You'll have a front-row seat to strategic decisions, learn how a mission-driven health care company operates at scale, and build relationships across every function of the organization. If you want a role where your work has a direct line to mission impact — this is it.

Responsibilities

Executive Support


Manage complex, dynamic calendars for multiple C-suite executives — proactively resolving conflicts, prioritizing requests, and anticipating scheduling needs before they arise
Coordinate domestic and international travel logistics including flights, hotels, ground transportation, and itineraries
Prepare executives for meetings by compiling briefing documents, agendas, background materials, and talking points
Handle correspondence, draft communications, and respond to inquiries on behalf of executives with appropriate judgment and discretion
Track action items and follow-ups from executive meetings to ensure nothing falls through the cracks


Operational & Administrative Excellence


Process expense reports and manage reimbursements accurately and on schedule
Coordinate logistics for leadership team meetings, offsites, board prep, and company-wide events — including venue sourcing, catering, tech setup, and materials
Maintain organized filing systems and document management for executive-level materials
Support onboarding for new senior leaders and manage relationships with key external stakeholders and vendors


Cross-Functional Coordination


Serve as a key point of contact across Brightline's teams on behalf of supported executives
Partner with other executive assistants and operations staff to ensure seamless coordination across the leadership team
Handle confidential and sensitive information with the highest level of integrity and discretion
Identify opportunities to streamline processes and improve the efficiency of executive operations


Requirements:


5+ years of experience as an executive assistant, ideally supporting C-suite leaders in a fast-paced, growth-stage environment
Exceptional organizational skills and a proven ability to manage competing priorities across multiple executives simultaneously
Strong written and verbal communication skills — comfortable drafting polished communications on behalf of senior leaders
High emotional intelligence and the ability to work effectively with a wide range of personalities and communication styles
Absolute discretion and sound judgment when handling sensitive, confidential information
Proficiency with Google Workspace (Gmail, Calendar, Drive, Docs, Sheets) and virtual meeting tools (Zoom, Slack)
Proactive, solutions-oriented mindset — you spot problems before they happen and come prepared with options
Preferred: Experience in healthcare, health tech, or a mission-driven startup environment

Stock Options

About Brightline

Brightline is a therapy and psychiatry practice that delivers expert pediatric, teen, and parental mental health care to families and kids up to age 18. Brightline’s virtual and in-person outpatient services include diagnostic evaluation, therapy, psychiatry services (e.g. medication management), and psychological testing (to assess learning differences, school readiness, executive functioning difficulties [e.g. ADHD], and autism). In addition to Brightline’s generalized support, we offer focused programs including those that support anxiety, obsessive compulsive disorders, ADHD, and disruptive behaviors. Founded in 2019, Brightline has delivered care to tens of thousands of families with industry-leading results. We’ve been nationally recognized for clinical excellence and innovation for several years — recent awards include the Fast Company 50 Most Innovative Companies (2022) and Behavioral Health Business Companies to Watch Award (2024). Brightline is based in Palo Alto and is backed by investors including Boston Children’s Hospital, Northwell Health, Blue Cross Blue Shield of Massachusetts, Google Ventures, KKR, and Oak HC/FT.
